About This Project

Why was this website created?

This website was created as a final project for English 381: Professional Writing and Technical Communication II at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. The content for this website was produced from February 2026 to May 2026.

The goal of this website is to provide information designers with a better understanding of digital accessibility.

How was the website's content created?

The content for this website consists of two parts. The first is my own individual contribution to this project, the Understanding Digital Accessibility chapter. The remaining chapters were created by students from English 380 in their individual groups, and focuses on creating accessible video content for the web. The only aspect of the team content that I produced was the 'How To Add Accessible Features in Clipchamp' section from the Chunking, Chapters, and On-Screen Markup chapter.

I reviewed and slightly edited the team content for proper spelling and grammar, but otherwise, the team content is the result of my classmates' work. I also used a different sourcing method to ensure consistency across teams (See 'Works Cited for Team Content').

I inputted both the individual and team content into this website using MadCap Flare, a technical writing software. Logos for this website were designed by myself using Adobe Illustrator, a graphic design software.

What concepts were emphasized when designing this website and its content?

Design concepts that I utilized in the creation of this website included:

  • Creating contrast through color and font choice

  • Using consistent and non-overlapping heading styles

  • Inputting alt text for all non-decorative images.

  • Chunking information and avoiding long paragraphs of text

  • Meeting accessibility standards for Level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.
